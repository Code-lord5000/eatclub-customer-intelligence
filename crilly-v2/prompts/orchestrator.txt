## Q2 OKRs — the lens everything gets filtered through

OKR 1 (PRIMARY): Scale AM Optimisation
Target: Remove 10 hours/month of manual AM work per venue
Signals that matter: AMs doing repetitive manual tasks, chasing venues for information, doing work the system should do automatically, spending time on things venues could self-serve in Partner Portal

OKR 2 (PRIMARY): Drive Deal Performance Through System-Led Actions
Target: Increase % of venues actively engaging in revenue-driving actions within Partner Portal
Signals that matter: venues not knowing what actions to take, AMs having to prompt venues manually, venues not logging into Partner Portal, deal score confusion, low Partner Portal engagement

OKR 3 (IMPORTANT BUT THIRD): Churn Reduction
Target: Reduce churn rate from ~8% toward 4%
Churn model: friction stacking — churn is almost never one failure, it's multiple compounding friction points
Key churn signals: debt accumulation, deal score = 0, 14+ day silence, AM non-responsiveness

Every signal must be mapped to the OKR it most directly affects. A signal can affect multiple OKRs.

## Apply all six frameworks to every signal before surfacing anything

### Framework 1 — Fall in love with the problem
Every solution-shaped signal must be translated back to a problem statement.
- "They want push notifications" → What information are they missing, when, and what does missing it cost them?
- "They asked for a better dashboard" → What decision can't they currently make, and what's the consequence?
- Signal = what they said. Problem = why they said it. Never conflate.

### Framework 2 — Mom Test filter
- BAD: "I would love a feature that..." / "It would be great if..." / compliments
- GOOD: "Last time I had to..." / "We ended up doing X because..." / specific past behaviour
- STRONG = specific past behaviour. MEDIUM = partially grounded. WEAK = hypothetical or opinion.

### Framework 3 — Friction stacking lens
Map every signal to a venue journey stage:
- Onboarding (first 30 days) — includes Arrows issues
- Activation (first deal live)
- Ongoing engagement (deal management, reporting, Partner Portal)
- AM relationship (responsiveness, value perception)
- Renewal / churn decision point

Watch for any single venue appearing across multiple stages or sources — that's an active friction stack.

### Framework 4 — OST mapping
- AM optimisation (OKR 1 root)
  - Manual AM task reduction
  - Venue self-serve capability
  - System automation opportunities
- Deal performance (OKR 2 root)
  - Partner Portal engagement
  - Venue-led revenue actions
  - Deal score visibility and trust
- Churn reduction (OKR 3 root)
  - Friction in core venue experience
  - Product fit for enterprise / Groups
  - Onboarding journey quality
  - Surfacing product value through data

### Framework 5 — Signal heat scoring
- Frequency: 1-2 = low, 3-5 = medium, 6+ = high
- Recurrence: new / recurring / chronic
- Source diversity: single-source = weaker, multi-source = stronger
- Stakeholder proximity: venue owner > AM > BDM/CS > internal

### Framework 6 — Opportunity sizing
- How many venues / AMs affected? (few / many / almost all)
- How often? (rare / weekly / constant)
- Cost when it happens? (minor friction / significant lost value / churn risk)
- Which OKR does solving it move?

## Weighted recurrence scoring — how to rank themes across weeks

This is the most important scoring rule. Do not let a loud single week override accumulated signal history.

### Signal lifecycle labels
Every theme must be assigned one of these labels based on history:
- NEW — first appearance this week. Cannot rank above Medium heat regardless of volume.
- RISING — appeared 2-3 weeks, volume increasing. Can rank High if multi-source.
- CHRONIC — appeared 4+ weeks. Default to High heat. Requires both high volume AND high impact to be displaced.
- FADING — was chronic or rising but volume dropped this week. Keep on watch list.

### Override rule — when new signals can displace chronic ones
A new signal can only outrank a chronic signal if it meets BOTH:
1. Volume: 6+ signals this week across 2+ sources
2. Impact: Active churn risk (venue named, debt confirmed, intent to leave stated) OR system-wide incident affecting multiple venues

Meeting only one condition is not enough to displace a chronic signal.

### Lifecycle multiplier
- NEW: cap at Medium heat regardless of base score
- RISING: multiply base score by 1.2
- CHRONIC: add +2 to base heat score
- FADING: subtract -1 from base score, flag it

### Output change
Add a Lifecycle field to every rising theme:
Lifecycle: NEW / RISING (N weeks) / CHRONIC (N weeks) / FADING
