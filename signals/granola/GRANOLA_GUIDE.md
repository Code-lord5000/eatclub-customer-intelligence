# Granola Meeting Signal Guide

Meetings with Pan, Sam, Luke, and Jeroen are high-value signal sources — but they require a different extraction discipline than Slack or HubSpot. The signal is usually buried inside strategic conversation, not stated explicitly as a problem.

## What you're listening for

### In Pan conversations
Pan operates at business outcome level. When he raises something, it's usually because he's heard it from multiple sources — venues, investors, or market observation. His signals are often:
- Competitive concerns ("I keep hearing about X")
- Revenue/churn patterns he's noticed in the numbers
- Strategic direction shifts that imply an underlying problem
- Things that are "taking too long" — usually a symptom of product friction upstream

**Extraction discipline**: When Pan brings a solution, ask yourself — what pattern of complaints or data would make a CEO say that? Log the implied problem, not the stated solution.

### In Luke conversations (Head of Account Management)
Luke is closest to AM experience — he hears what AMs are struggling with day-to-day, what venues are saying in calls, and where AMs are spending time they shouldn't be. His signals are:
- AM workflow friction ("the team keeps having to manually...")
- Venue sentiment at renewal ("venues are saying...")
- Operational workarounds AMs have invented
- Escalations that keep recurring

**Extraction discipline**: Luke's signals are often already at problem level — but they're framed from the AM's perspective, not the venue's. Always ask: what's the downstream effect on the venue?

### In Sam conversations
Sam sees product from a commercial and delivery lens. His signals often relate to:
- Features that aren't being adopted despite being built
- Gaps between what was promised and what was delivered
- Market or segment problems that aren't being addressed

### In Jeroen conversations
Jeroen's signals tend to be data-adjacent — things visible in Mixpanel or operational metrics that haven't been surfaced as problems yet.

## Extraction template for Granola notes

After each relevant meeting, add a block to the weekly Granola signal file:

```
### Meeting: {Pan / Luke / Sam / Jeroen} — {date}

**Raw signal**:
> [exact quote or close paraphrase — mark (paraphrase) if not exact]

**Context**: What was being discussed when this came up?

**Implied problem** (your translation, not theirs):

**Solution they attached to it** (if any):

**Mom Test quality**: STRONG / MEDIUM / WEAK
- STRONG: they referenced a specific past event, customer, or data point
- MEDIUM: general observation with some grounding
- WEAK: hypothetical, opinion, or solution-first framing

**Follow-up questions I should ask**:
```

## The key discipline

When a stakeholder brings a solution in a meeting, your job in that moment is to **not engage with the solution** — engage with the problem underneath it.

Questions that work:
- "What's making that a priority right now — what are you seeing?"
- "Tell me about a specific venue or AM where this has been an issue"
- "What happens currently when [X situation] comes up?"

These questions turn a solution conversation into a signal conversation. Log what they say in response — that's your strongest data.
